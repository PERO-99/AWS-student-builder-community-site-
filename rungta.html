Rungta University Schools

Established in 1999 under the GDR Educational Society for Higher & Technical Education by Shri Ghanshyam Dasji Rungta, Rungta College began as a single engineering institute. Under the visionary leadership of Group Chairman Santosh Rungta, it expanded into a renowned educational group comprising four colleges in Engineering, Pharmacy, Commerce, and Science. In 2025, with official government approval, it transformed into Rungta International Skills University—Central India’s first private skill-focused university. Located on an 80-acre campus in Bhilai, it offers NAAC A Grade accredited, industry-aligned UG, PG, and doctoral programs across disciplines with global certifications from Google, Microsoft, and IBM.

Rungotsav Film Festival 2026

Rungta International Skills University, Bhilai, successfully organized Rungotsav Film Festival 2026, a vibrant two-day celebration of cinema, storytelling, creativity, and industry interaction. Hosted by the School of Film & Media, the festival brought together acclaimed filmmakers, actors, writers, producers, digital creators, and cinema enthusiasts under one roof, creating an inspiring platform for learning, networking, and creative exchange.

Designed around the theme “Idea to Screen”, Rungotsav provided students and aspiring filmmakers with a unique opportunity to interact directly with renowned personalities from the Indian film industry while exploring the art and business of filmmaking.

Celebrated Film Personalities Graced the Festival

The festival featured several distinguished guests whose remarkable contributions to Indian cinema inspired the audience through engaging interactions and insightful discussions.

Chitrashi Rawat

Widely loved for portraying Komal Chautala in the blockbuster film Chak De! India, actress Chitrashi Rawat interacted with students and shared her inspiring journey from sports to cinema. She encouraged aspiring artists to remain dedicated, embrace challenges, and continuously refine their craft.

Vivek Sharma

Acclaimed filmmaker Vivek Sharma, best known for directing the Bollywood blockbuster Bhoothnath, shared valuable insights into filmmaking, direction, script interpretation, and working with legendary actors. His session offered students a practical understanding of commercial cinema and the responsibilities of a film director.

Apurva Dhar Badgaiyan

Renowned writer and director Apurva Dhar Badgaiyan, known for his work on Mirzapur and Chaman Bahar, delivered an engaging session on screenplay writing, storytelling, OTT platforms, and creating compelling narratives. His interaction gave aspiring writers a deeper understanding of contemporary content creation.

Kiann Vashodia

Actor Kiann Vashodia, recognized for his work in films and digital entertainment, motivated students by discussing acting techniques, auditions, and the growing opportunities available across OTT and digital platforms.

Dhruvaditya Bhagwanani

Actor and producer Dhruvaditya Bhagwanani, known for Gangs of Raipur, encouraged young filmmakers to create authentic stories rooted in originality while sharing practical experiences from independent filmmaking.

Honouring the Pride of Chhattisgarhi Cinema

Rungotsav also celebrated the remarkable achievements of regional cinema by welcoming some of the most respected names from the Chhattisgarh film industry.

Padma Shri Anuj Sharma

One of the biggest icons of Chhattisgarhi cinema, Padma Shri Anuj Sharma inspired the audience with his remarkable journey as an actor, singer, and public figure. His interaction highlighted the growing recognition of regional cinema on the national stage.

Satish Jain

Veteran filmmaker Satish Jain, regarded as one of the pioneers of modern Chhattisgarhi cinema, shared his experiences in filmmaking and emphasized the importance of preserving regional stories through meaningful cinema.

Manoj Verma

Accomplished filmmaker Manoj Verma interacted with aspiring creators and discussed filmmaking techniques, production challenges, and opportunities in regional cinema.

Manish Manikpuri

Director Manish Manikpuri spoke about independent filmmaking, creative vision, and the future of regional storytelling, inspiring students to pursue original narratives.

Pitch Lab: Turning Creative Ideas into Reality

One of the major attractions of the festival was the Rungotsav Pitch Lab, an exclusive initiative that provided aspiring storytellers with the opportunity to present their original ideas directly before experienced industry professionals.

Participants pitched concepts for:

  • Short Films
  • Feature Films
  • Web Series
  • Documentaries
  • Digital Content
  • Vertical Dramas

The platform enabled filmmakers, writers, students, and content creators to receive valuable industry feedback, mentorship, networking opportunities, and guidance for developing promising ideas into future productions.

Digital Creators Added a New Dimension

The festival also welcomed several prominent digital creators and social media influencers who amplified the event’s reach through their online communities.

Among them were:

  • Pijush K Roy (@hiraipur)
  • Adarsh Mishra (@raipurfoodielove)
  • Dr. Kirti Sisodia
  • Yashwant Sahu (Bhilai Times)
  • Minendra Chandrakar

Their participation helped connect the festival with a broader digital audience while encouraging young creators to explore opportunities in content creation and digital storytelling.

An Unforgettable Learning Experience

Over two days, students experienced:

  • Celebrity interactions
  • Masterclasses
  • Panel discussions
  • Film screenings
  • Networking sessions
  • Storytelling workshops
  • Pitch presentations

The festival provided invaluable exposure by allowing students to engage directly with experienced professionals who have shaped both mainstream and regional cinema.

Nurturing the Next Generation of Storytellers

With its successful conclusion, Rungotsav Film Festival 2026 reaffirmed Rungta International Skills University’s commitment to experiential learning, creative excellence, and industry collaboration. The event strengthened the University’s vision of preparing future filmmakers, writers, actors, and content creators through meaningful interaction with accomplished professionals.

Rungotsav continues to emerge as a unique platform where education meets industry, empowering young storytellers to transform their ideas into impactful cinematic experiences.

Entrepreneurship 101

Entrepreneurship 101 Inspires Students at Rungta University to Embrace the Realities of Startup Life

Rungta University, through its Rungta Business Incubator (RuBI), successfully organized Entrepreneurship 101 on 22 May 2026 at the B Block Auditorium, bringing together aspiring entrepreneurs, innovators, and students eager to understand the realities of building a startup.

The keynote session was delivered by Mr. Pradeep Yuvaraj, Business Storytelling Specialist, Pitchdeck Strategist, Keynote Speaker, Global Co-Lead – TiE-U, and Vice-President, TiE Coimbatore. With extensive entrepreneurial experience that includes three startup failures, two successful exits, and one acquihire, he shared practical insights and honest lessons from his entrepreneurial journey.

The session, titled “A Hundred Reasons Not to Become an Entrepreneur and One Reason Why You Should,” focused on the challenges, uncertainties, and responsibilities that come with entrepreneurship. Moving beyond the glamor often associated with startup culture, Mr. Yuvaraj encouraged students to understand the importance of resilience, adaptability, problem-solving, and continuous learning.

Students gained valuable exposure to topics such as startup validation, Lean Canvas, founder networking, mentorship, storytelling, and the importance of building solutions with purpose. The session also highlighted how failures and setbacks are essential learning experiences in the entrepreneurial journey.

Addressing a packed auditorium, the speaker emphasized that entrepreneurship is not about avoiding failure, but about learning from every challenge and continuing to move forward with determination and vision. His engaging interaction with students inspired many young minds to think beyond conventional career paths and explore innovation-driven opportunities.

The event served as a meaningful platform for students to interact with an industry expert and gain real-world understanding of the startup ecosystem. Entrepreneurship 101 reinforced Rungta University’s commitment to nurturing innovation, leadership, and entrepreneurial thinking among students.

The session concluded with an interactive discussion where students actively participated, asked questions, and explored ideas related to startups, leadership, and future business opportunities.

QS I-GAUGE Round Table 2026

On the occasion of National Technology Day celebrated on 11 May 2026, Rungta International Skills University successfully organized the QS I-GAUGE Round Table 2026, bringing together distinguished academicians, education leaders, and industry experts for insightful discussions on the future of higher education, innovation, and employability.

Held under the theme “Shaping the Future of Higher Education, Innovation & Industry Readiness,” the event served as a dynamic platform for exchanging ideas and exploring strategies that can strengthen the connection between academia and industry. The round table focused on preparing students for rapidly evolving professional environments through innovation-driven education, practical learning models, and industry collaboration.

The event witnessed the presence of eminent personalities including Dr. Rajiv Prakash, Shri Ravin Nair, Shri Somesh Sharma, and Dr. Jawahar Surisetti. Their discussions emphasized the growing importance of skill-based learning, emerging technologies, entrepreneurship, and innovation-focused academic ecosystems.

The participants shared valuable insights on how educational institutions can align academic curricula with industry expectations to improve student employability and global competitiveness. Discussions also highlighted the importance of research collaborations, technology integration, and experiential learning in shaping future-ready graduates.

A major focus of the event remained on building stronger industry-academia partnerships that provide students with real-world exposure and practical problem-solving opportunities. The speakers stressed that institutions must continuously evolve their teaching methodologies and innovation frameworks to meet the demands of modern industries and technological advancements.

The round table also explored the role of universities in promoting entrepreneurship, creativity, leadership, and multidisciplinary learning. Experts discussed how collaborative ecosystems between industries and educational institutions can create meaningful career pathways and encourage students to become innovators and changemakers.

The successful organization of the QS I-GAUGE Round Table 2026 on National Technology Day reflected Rungta International Skills University’s commitment toward fostering innovation, academic excellence, and industry readiness. The event provided a valuable platform for thought leaders to share ideas and collectively shape the future of higher education.

With initiatives like these, the university continues to strengthen its vision of creating globally competent professionals equipped with knowledge, technological skills, and innovation-driven mindsets for the future.

Sach Hue Sapne - Grand Closing Ceremony Placementnamaa 2026

The Story

What began as a dream to transform lives through opportunity reached its glorious crescendo on 28 April 2026 as Rungta International Skills University hosted the Grand Closing Ceremony of Placement Namaa 2026 : Sach Hue Sapne. The evening was graced by the august presence of Hon’ble Governor of Chhattisgarh, Shri Ramen Deka Ji, who personally handed offer letters to the placed students  a moment that transformed paper into purpose, and effort into destiny.

What Made It Historic

Placement Namaa 2026 was not just a campus placement drive — it was a movement. In a first-of-its-kind initiative, the drive opened its doors beyond the campus, welcoming students from other colleges across the region, making it a truly inclusive opportunity for aspiring professionals. The scale of participation spoke volumes: 121 companies brought real roles, real salaries, and real futures to 10,842 registered students.

The result? A record 1,702 unique selections — each one a story, each one a dream fulfilled. The highest package of ₹1

7 LPA  set a new benchmark, while selections ranging from ₹6 LPA to ₹15.8 LPA ensured opportunities across every aspiration and ambition.

 

“Numbers tell the scale. But the joy on every selected student’s face — that tells the story.”

Placement Namaa 2026 · Grand Closing Ceremony

 

The Closing Note

The Grand Closing Ceremony was more than a celebration ; it was a message. A message that when an institution believes in its students, when industry trusts its talent, and when a community opens its doors to all dreamers, extraordinary things happen. Rungta University’s 14 years of successful placements culminated in this golden evening ; an evening of recognition, gratitude, and the quiet, powerful sound of Sach Hue Sapne.
